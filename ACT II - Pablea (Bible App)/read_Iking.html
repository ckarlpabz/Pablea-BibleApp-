I King

Solomon's princes and officers

Chapter 4:1-20

I King 4:1: So king Solomon was king over all Israel.
I King 4:2: And these were the princes which he had; Azariah the son of Zadok the priest,
I King 4:3: Elihoreph and Ahiah, the sons of Shisha, scribes; Jehoshaphat the son of Ahilud, the recorder.
I King 4:4: And Benaiah the son of Jehoiada was over the host: and Zadok and Abiathar were the priests:
I King 4:5: And Azariah the son of Nathan was over the officers: and Zabud the son of Nathan was principal officer, and the king's friend:
I King 4:6: And Ahishar was over the household: and Adoniram the son of Abda was over the tribute.
I King 4:7: And Solomon had twelve officers over all Israel, which provided victuals for the king and his household: each man his month in a year made provision.
I King 4:8: And these are their names: The son of Hur, in mount Ephraim:
I King 4:9: The son of Dekar, in Makaz, and in Shaalbim, and Beth-shemesh, and Elon-beth-hanan:
I King 4:10: The son of Hesed, in Aruboth; to him pertained Sochoh, and all the land of Hepher:
I King 4:11: The son of Abinadab, in all the region of Dor; which had Taphath the daughter of Solomon to wife:
I King 4:12: Baana the son of Ahilud; to him pertained Taanach and Megiddo, and all Beth-shean, which is by Zartanah beneath Jezreel, from Beth-shean to Abel-meholah, even unto the place that is beyond Jokneam:
I King 4:13: The son of Geber, in Ramoth-gilead; to him pertained the towns of Jair the son of Manasseh, which are in Gilead; to him also pertained the region of Argob, which is in Bashan, threescore great cities with walls and brasen bars:
I King 4:14: Ahinadab the son of Iddo had Mahanaim:
I King 4:15: Ahimaaz was in Naphtali; he also took Basmath the daughter of Solomon to wife:
I King 4:16: Baanah the son of Hushai was in Asher and in Aloth:
I King 4:17: Jehoshaphat the son of Paruah, in Issachar:
I King 4:18: Shimei the son of Elah, in Benjamin:
I King 4:19: Geber the son of Uri was in the country of Gilead, in the country of Sihon king of the Amorites, and of Og king of Bashan; and he was the only officer which was in the land.
I King 4:20: Judah and Israel were many, as the sand which is by the sea in multitude, eating and drinking, and making merry.

Solomon's daily provision

Chapter 4:21-28

I King 4:21: And Solomon reigned over all kingdoms from the river unto the land of the Philistines, and unto the border of Egypt: they brought presents, and served Solomon all the days of his life.
I King 4:22: And Solomon's provision for one day was thirty measures of fine flour, and threescore measures of meal,
I King 4:23: Ten fat oxen, and twenty oxen out of the pastures, and an hundred sheep, beside harts, and roebucks, and fallowdeer, and fatted fowl.
I King 4:24: For he had dominion over all the region on this side the river, from Tiphsah even to Azzah, over all the kings on this side the river: and he had peace on all sides round about him.
I King 4:25: And Judah and Israel dwelt safely, every man under his vine and under his fig tree, from Dan even to Beer-sheba, all the days of Solomon.
I King 4:26: And Solomon had forty thousand stalls of horses for his chariots, and twelve thousand horsemen.
I King 4:27: And those officers provided victual for king Solomon, and for all that came unto king Solomon's table, every man in his month: they lacked nothing.
I King 4:28: Barley also and straw for the horses and dromedaries brought they unto the place where the officers were, every man according to his charge.
